Generation I

In the period of the year 1940-1956, it was referred to as the period of the first generation of computers. These machines are slow, huge, and expensive. In this generation of computers, vacuum tubes were used as the basic components of CPU and memory. Also, they were mainly dependent on the batch operating systems and punch cards. Magnetic tape and paper tape were used as output and input devices. For example ENIAC, UNIVAC-1, EDVAC, etc. Below is the image of UNIVAC-1 computer.

UNIVAC

UNIVAC-1 (Universal Automatic Computer) was the first commercially produced computer in the United States. Developed by J. Presper Eckert and John Mauchly, who were also involved in creating ENIAC, UNIVAC-1 marked a significant advancement in computer technology and played a crucial role in shaping the early computing industry.
